There are two main types of treatment for depression: talking therapies such as counselling and cognitive-behavioural therapy (CBT) and antidepressant medication. There are also several things that can help people to look after themselves:

  • Seek help: Talk to your doctor or another health professional or a school/university counsellor to get the best help.
  • Talk to someone: It is a good idea to not keep problems and upsets to yourself – talking to someone close to you, and telling them how you feel, sharing your concerns, and asking for help can help to come to terms with upsetting events.
  • Do something: Second, it is a good idea to be active. Being physically active can improve mood, and exercise can itself have antidepressant effects. Getting outside and seeing the natural world can be benefical. As best you can, try and do things that you enjoy and value. It is easy to stop doing positive things when depressed, so it is a good idea to build these up. Try and build things up in small steps.
  • Look after yourself physically: As best you can, try to eat regularly and healthily. When feeling down, it is easy to either skip meals or eat badly, which only worsens how you feel. Similarly, be careful with alcohol and drugs like cannabis – many people use alcohol and drugs to relax and numb their distress, but they actually make depression worse. Try and have a regular sleep routine – going to bed at the same time and getting up at the same time.
  • Connect with others: When feeling down, it is easy to withdraw from others. Mixing with others and getting involved with friends and family can help your mood. Helping other people can also improve your mood.
  • Tackle problems: If there is a underlying difficulty behind your depression, trying to solve this problem might help. Write down the problem, all the different things you could to tackle it and then try the option that is most likely to help.
  • Stay hopeful: Remember that many other people have had depression. Whilst it may not feel like it, you will eventually feel better – most people recover and there are helpful treatments.
